Healing Duas - Ruqyah for Physical Ailments

Seeking Shifa (Cure) from Allah, The Ultimate Healer

The Spiritual Path for Healing

This page presents a comprehensive collection of Du'as and Ruqyah (Qur'anic healing) for seeking cure from physical ailments and pain. All healing comes from Allah alone, and these supplications are powerful means to seek His mercy and cure. The practice follows the Prophetic method of reciting specific Du'as and then gently blowing on the affected area or the person who is ill.

1. Beginning with Praise of Allah

Start by praising Allah and affirming His greatness, as this is the foundation of all supplication.

Instruction: Recite each surah starting with Bismillāhi ar-Raḥmāni ar-Raḥīm:
Sūrah Al-Fātiḥah
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ ۝١ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ ۝٢ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ ۝٣ مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ ۝٤ إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ ۝٥ اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ ۝٦ صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ ۝٧
Sūrah Al-Ikhlāṣ
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ ۝١ اللَّهُ الصَّمَدُ ۝٢ لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُولَدْ ۝٣ وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ ۝٤
Sūrah Al-Falaq
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ ۝١ مِن شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ ۝٢ وَمِن شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ إِذَا وَقَبَ ۝٣ وَمِن شَرِّ النَّفَّاثَاتِ فِي الْعُقَدِ ۝٤ وَمِن شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ ۝٥
Sūrah An-Nās
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ ۝١ مَلِكِ النَّاسِ ۝٢ إِلَٰهِ النَّاسِ ۝٣ مِن شَرِّ الْوَسْوَاسِ الْخَنَّاسِ ۝٤ الَّذِي يُوَسْوِسُ فِي صُدُورِ النَّاسِ ۝٥ مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ ۝٦
(Reciting each once is sufficient; repeat 3x if you wish to follow the full Sunnah practice.)
لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ الْعَظِيمُ الْحَلِيمُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ رَبُّ الْعَرْشِ الْعَظِيمِ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ رَبُّ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَرَبُّ الْأَرْضِ وَرَبُّ الْعَرْشِ الْكَرِيمِ
Translation: "There is no deity worthy of worship except Allah, the Magnificent, the Forbearing. There is no deity worthy of worship except Allah, Lord of the Great Throne. There is no deity worthy of worship except Allah, Lord of the heavens, Lord of the earth, and Lord of the Noble Throne."

2. Seeking Protection with Allah's Perfect Words

These powerful Du'as seek refuge in Allah's complete and perfect words from all harm.

أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّاتِ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ
Translation: "I seek refuge in the perfect words of Allah from the evil of what He has created."

Reference: Sahih Muslim
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الَّذِي لَا يَضُرُّ مَعَ اسْمِهِ شَيْءٌ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَا فِي السَّمَاءِ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
Translation: "In the name of Allah, with whose name nothing on earth or in the sky can cause harm, and He is the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing."

Reference: Sunan Abu Dawud

3. General Duas for Healing and Well-being

These comprehensive supplications ask Allah for overall well-being and protection from harm.

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ العَافِيَةَ فِي الدُّنْيَا وَالآخِرَةِ
Translation: "O Allah, I ask You for well-being (al-'afiyah) in this world and the Hereafter."

Reference: Sunan Ibn Majah
أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللهِ التَّامَّةِ مِنْ غَضَبِهِ، وَعِقَابِهِ، وَشَرِّ عِبَادِهِ، وَمِنْ هَمَزَاتِ الشَّيَاطِينِ، وَأَنْ يَحْضُرُونِ
Translation: "I seek refuge in the perfect words of Allah from His anger, His punishment, the evil of His servants, from the promptings of the devils, and that they should be present with me."

Reference: Sunan Abu Dawud

4. Specific Ruqyah for Pain and Illness

These are direct supplications taught by the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) for healing physical ailments.

If reciting for someone else say
بِاسْمِ اللهِ يُبْرِيكْ وَمِنْ كُلِّ دَاءٍ يَشْفِيكْ وَمِنْ شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ وَمِنْ شَرِّ كُلِّ ذِي عَيْنٍ
If reciting for your self say
بِاسْمِ اللهِ يُبْرِينِي وَمِنْ كُلِّ دَاءٍ يَشْفِيَنِي وَمِنْ شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ وَمِنْ شَرِّ كُلِّ ذِي عَيْنٍ
Translation: "In the name of Allah, may He make you healthy, and from every illness may He heal you, and from the evil of an envier when he envies, and from the evil of every possessor of the evil eye."

Reference: Sahih Muslim
If reciting for someone else say
أَسْأَلُ اللهَ الْعَظِيمَ رَبَّ الْعَرْشِ الْعَظِيمِ أَنْ يَشْفِيكْ
If reciting for your self say
أَسْأَلُ اللهَ الْعَظِيمَ رَبَّ الْعَرْشِ الْعَظِيمِ أَنْ يَشْفِيَنِي
Translation: "I ask Allah the Magnificent, Lord of the Magnificent Throne, to heal you."

Reference: Sunan Abu Dawud
بِسْمِ اللهِ (ثلاث مرات) أَعُوذُ بِاللهِ وَقُدْرَتِهِ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا أَجِدُ وَأُحَاذِرُ (سبع مرات)
Translation: "In the name of Allah (3 times). I seek refuge in Allah and His power from the evil of what I feel and what I guard against." (7 times)

Reference: Sahih Muslim

5. Powerful Conclusion Duas

End your Ruqyah with these comprehensive and powerful supplications for complete healing.

اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ النَّاسِ أَذْهِبِ الْبَاسَ، اشْفِهِ وَأَنْتَ الشَّافِي، لاَ شِفَاءَ إِلاَّ شِفَاؤُكَ، شِفَاءً لاَ يُغَادِرُ سَقَمًا
Translation: "O Allah, Lord of mankind, remove the harm and heal him, for You are the Healer. There is no healing except Your healing, a healing that leaves no illness behind."

Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari
اللَّهُمَّ اشْفِهِ شِفَاءً لَا يُغَادِرُ سَقَمًا
Translation: "O Allah, heal him with a cure that leaves no illness."

Reference: Based on the Prophetic supplication

Recommended Practice for Ruqyah

How to Perform Ruqyah Correctly:

  • Begin with Sincerity - Have firm belief that cure comes from Allah alone.
  • Recite with Presence of Mind - Focus on the meaning of the words you're reciting.
  • Blow Gently - After reciting, blow gently (with a tiny bit of saliva) on your hands and wipe over the affected area or the person who is ill.
  • Use the Right Hand - The Prophet (ﷺ) used his right hand for wiping during Ruqyah.
  • Repeat as Needed - You can repeat the Ruqyah multiple times, especially the shorter Du'as.
  • Combine with Medical Treatment - Ruqyah complements medical treatment; it does not replace it. The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Seek treatment, O servants of Allah."
  • Have Certainty - Recite with yaqeen (certainty) that Allah can and will respond to your supplication.

Note: The effectiveness of Ruqyah depends on sincerity, correct belief, and adherence to the Sunnah. Avoid any practices that involve shirk (associating partners with Allah) or innovations.
