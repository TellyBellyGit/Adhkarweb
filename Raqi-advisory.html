How to Spot a Genuine Raqi (and Avoid a False One)

A guide to distinguishing authentic Islamic spiritual healing from practices that involve shirk or deception. Click an item to view details.

Recitation
Genuine: Recites only authentic Qur'anic verses and prophetic duʿas, clearly and correctly in Arabic. May explain the meanings to help the patient understand what is being read.
False: Mumbles in an unknown language, mixes foreign or "mystical" words, or uses chants that are not from Qur'an or Sunnah. Sometimes uses "secret names" of jinn or angels, coded syllables, or meaningless sounds to hide shirk.
Interaction with Female Patients
Genuine: Respects Islamic guidelines — does not touch or physically interact with non-mahram women. Keeps a professional and modest distance.
False: Suggests touching a woman as part of "treatment" — even if he appears to be an old sheikh with a white beard and a calm, holy demeanour. This is a major red flag. Such men exploit women at their most vulnerable. Do not confront this snake — just leave. In some cases, touching is used as a cover for sexual abuse or transferring sihr/jinn contact.
Knowledge of the Unseen
Genuine: Never claims to know the unseen, the future, or hidden personal matters. Makes assessments only from what is visible or what the patient shares.
False: Claims special knowledge — predicting the future, telling you hidden details about your life, or describing events they could not naturally know. This is usually through communication with jinn, who pass information in exchange for acts of shirk and filth. These can include desecrating the Qur'an, engaging in immoral acts, or performing rituals for the jinn's benefit.
Items Requested
Genuine: May ask for simple, reasonable items such as water, olive oil, or honey for ruqyah.
False: Requests strange or impossible items — such as a black bird, three-legged goat, or rare herbs with no Islamic basis. Asks for personal items you've worn or touched, especially intimate clothing (e.g., underwear) or hair/nails. This is a well-known magician tactic, often used to create or strengthen sihr against the victim.
Approach to Healing
Genuine: Encourages tawakkul (reliance on Allah), patience, and combining ruqyah with medical treatment when needed. Advises self-ruqyah so the patient learns to protect themselves.
False: Makes you dependent on him, discourages self-ruqyah, and claims only he can "cure" you. Sometimes sets up long, expensive "treatment packages" with no end date.
Amulets and Charms
Genuine: Never gives taweez, amulets, or charms to wear, burn, bury, or hang. Teaches that healing comes through Allah's words, not objects.
False: Gives such items and claims they will protect or heal you. Often these contain squares, numbers, and symbols with no meaning in Arabic; names of unknown "spirits" or "angels" (often jinn); verses of Qur'an written backwards or mixed with strange symbols; knots, threads, powders, or animal parts used in occult rituals; and instructions to burn, bury, dissolve in water and drink, or hang at certain locations. Such practices are forbidden and often involve cooperation with jinn. A genuine raqi will tell you to destroy these items safely (e.g., by soaking in water until the ink fades, then disposing respectfully).
Behaviour and Environment
Genuine: Is humble, patient, and works openly. Keeps treatment in a safe, well-lit, and non-isolated setting.
False: Boasts of "special powers" or unique status. Orders the patient to isolate for days, remain in darkness, or avoid all human contact without Islamic proof. Such isolation is often a way to break the patient's mental resilience and make them more suggestible.
Superstitious Practices
Genuine: Bases all actions on Qur'an and Sunnah, avoiding superstition.
False: Matches your name (and sometimes your mother's name) with elements like earth, wind, water, or fire — a practice from occult numerology, not Islam. May also tell you your "star sign" or birth month affects your treatment.
Important Note
A genuine raqi will always emphasize the importance of faith in Allah alone, adherence to Islamic principles, and seeking proper medical treatment when necessary. If you encounter any of the "false" practices described above, distance yourself immediately and seek guidance from knowledgeable, trustworthy scholars.
All ruqyah treatment MUST BE based on Quran and Sunnah.
