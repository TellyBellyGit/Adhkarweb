A Simple Guide to Self Ruqyah

Based on the teachings of Tim Humble - For personal collection and study purposes

What is Ruqyah?

Ruqyah is the treatment of sicknesses and afflictions through the recitation of the Quran, the Names of Allah and the authentic dua and adhkaar that are reported from the Prophet ﷺ.

It can be used for any kind of sickness or affliction, anything for which you seek a cure. Whether it is psychological, physical, mental, medical, financial, jinn, sihr, evil eye, magic, jealously. Whatever has afflicted you, ruqyah can be a cure بإذن الله تبارك وتعالى.

وَلَيْسَ بِضَآرِّهِمْ شَيْـًٔا إِلَّا بِإِذْنِ ٱللَّهِۚ وَعَلَى ٱللَّهِ فَلْيَتَوَكَّلِ ٱلْمُؤْمِنُونَ
"But he will not harm them at all except by permission of Allah. And upon Allah let the believers rely." [Al-Mujadila: Ayah 10]

7 Essential Points Before Ruqyah

There are 7 essential points that form the bases of getting out of every museebah (calamity) and that comes before seeking to do Ruqyah:

  1. 1. Correct your Aqeedah

    The Six Pillars of Iman: Explanation & Modern Negation

    1. Belief in Allah

    Correct Belief:
    To believe Allah is One, without partners. He is the Creator, the only One deserving of all worship. We affirm His Names & Attributes without distorting them.

    Some Beliefs & Actions That Contradict This Pillar:

    • Calling upon saints, graves, or spirits for help.
    • Using charms/amulets for protection instead of trusting Allah.
    • Living a life of disobedience as if Allah will not hold us accountable.

    2. Belief in the Angels

    Correct Belief:
    To believe in the existence of the angels as noble servants of Allah created from light. They carry out His commands perfectly.

    Some Beliefs & Actions That Contradict This Pillar:

    • Denying their existence or attributing events to mere “luck” or “coincidence.”
    • Consulting fortune-tellers (who claim to access the unseen, a realm belonging to Allah).

    3. Belief in the Revealed Books

    Correct Belief:
    To believe that Allah sent down divine scriptures, including the Torah, Gospel, and the Qur'an—the final, preserved, and unaltered revelation.

    Some Beliefs & Actions That Contradict This Pillar:

    • Denying the Qur'an's divine origin.
    • Following only the parts of the Qur'an that suit one's desires and ignoring the rest.
    • Believing the current versions of previous scriptures are still pure and unaltered.

    4. Belief in the Messengers

    Correct Belief:
    To believe that Allah sent prophets from Adam to Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and finally Muhammad (peace be upon them all). Muhammad (ﷺ) is the final prophet.

    Some Beliefs & Actions That Contradict This Pillar:

    • Denying the finality of Prophet Muhammad's (ﷺ) message.
    • Disrespecting any of the prophets.
    • Claiming “all paths lead to God,” nullifying the need for Allah's messengers.

    5. Belief in the Last Day

    Correct Belief:
    To believe in the Resurrection, the Day of Judgment, the weighing of deeds, and Paradise and Hellfire as final and eternal destinations.

    Some Beliefs & Actions That Contradict This Pillar:

    • Living solely for this world, with no concern for accountability in the Hereafter.
    • Believing death is the final end, with no resurrection or reckoning.

    6. Belief in Divine Decree (Qadr)

    Correct Belief:
    To believe that everything—good and bad—occurs by Allah's will, knowledge, and pre-ordainment.

    Some Beliefs & Actions That Contradict This Pillar:

    • Blaming “fate” for one's own sins and poor choices, avoiding responsibility.
    • Becoming arrogant about success, attributing it solely to one’s efforts and forgetting Allah's decree.
  2. 2. Tawbah (Repentance)

    Turn to Allah in sincere repentance. Allah says:

    وَتُوبُوا إِلَى اللَّهِ جَمِيعًا أَيُّهَا الْمُؤْمِنُونَ لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ
    "And turn to Allah in repentance, all of you, O believers, that you might succeed." [An-Nur: 31]

    The conditions of sincere repentance (Tawbah Nasooh) are:

    • Regret for the sin committed
    • Seeking forgiveness from Allah
    • Resolving never to return to the sin
    • If the sin involved wronging others, seeking their forgiveness and making amends
  3. 3. Remove the Means of Disobedience

    Remove from your life anything that leads to disobedience to Allah. This includes:

    • Haram music and entertainment
    • Inappropriate images and videos
    • Bad company that leads you away from Allah
    • Any objects or practices related to shirk or innovation

    The Prophet ﷺ said:

    "Whoever gives up something for the sake of Allah, Allah will compensate him with something better." [Ahmad]
  4. 4. Increase in Righteous Deeds

    Increase your good deeds and acts of worship:

    • Establish the five daily prayers on time
    • Read the Quran regularly with contemplation
    • Make dhikr (remembrance of Allah) frequently
    • Give charity and help others
    • Fast voluntarily (Mondays, Thursdays, white days)
    • Perform night prayers (Tahajjud)
    إِنَّ الْحَسَنَاتِ يُذْهِبْنَ السَّيِّئَاتِ
    "Indeed, good deeds do away with misdeeds." [Hud: 114]
  5. 5. Make Dua

    Make sincere dua to Allah, especially during the blessed times:

    • Between Maghrib and Isha
    • The last third of the night
    • Between Asr and Maghrib on Friday
    • While fasting
    • After the obligatory prayers

    Use the authentic duas from the Quran and Sunnah, and also make dua in your own words from your heart.

  6. 6. Have Good Expectations of Allah

    Always have husn al-dhann (good expectations) of Allah. The Prophet ﷺ said:

    "Allah says: 'I am as My servant thinks I am.'" [Bukhari and Muslim]

    Believe that Allah will cure you, help you, and relieve your distress. Have certainty that Allah's mercy encompasses all things.

  7. 7. Seek Forgiveness (Istighfar)

    Constantly seek Allah's forgiveness. The Prophet ﷺ said:

    "Whoever persists in seeking forgiveness, Allah will grant him relief from every distress and a way out from every hardship, and will provide for him from where he does not expect." [Abu Dawud]

    The best form of seeking forgiveness is Sayyid al-Istighfar:

    اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لاَ إِلَهَ إِلاَّ أَنْتَ، خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ، وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ، أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ، أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَىَّ، وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِر لِي فَإِنَّهُ لاَ يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلاَّ أَنْتَ
    "O Allah, You are my Lord, there is no god but You. You created me and I am Your servant. I abide by Your covenant and promise as much as I can. I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your blessing upon me and I acknowledge my sin, so forgive me, for indeed none can forgive sins except You."

How to Perform Self Ruqyah

Step 1: Prepare Yourself

  • Make wudu (ablution)
  • Face the qiblah if possible
  • Choose a quiet, clean place
  • Have sincere intention (niyyah) to seek healing from Allah alone

Step 2: Begin with Praise and Seeking Refuge

Start by praising Allah and seeking refuge:

أَعُوذُ بِاللَّهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ
"I seek refuge in Allah from Satan, the accursed."
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ
"In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful."

Ruqyah for Protection from Evil (Sahih Muslim)

Note: Choose the appropriate version based on who you are performing ruqyah for:

For performing ruqyah on others (male)
بِاسْمِ اللَّهِ أَرْقِيكَ مِنْ كُلِّ شَىْءٍ يُؤْذِيكَ مِنْ شَرِّ كُلِّ نَفْسٍ أَوْ عَيْنِ حَاسِدٍ اللَّهُ يَشْفِيكَ بِاسْمِ اللَّهِ أَرْقِيكَ
For performing ruqyah on others (female)
بِاسْمِ اللَّهِ أَرْقِيكِ مِنْ كُلِّ شَىْءٍ يُؤْذِيكِ مِنْ شَرِّ كُلِّ نَفْسٍ أَوْ عَيْنِ حَاسِدٍ اللَّهُ يَشْفِيكِ بِاسْمِ اللَّهِ أَرْقِيكِ
For performing ruqyah on yourself
بِاسْمِ اللَّهِ أَرْقِي نَفْسِي مِنْ كُلِّ شَىْءٍ يُؤْذِينِي مِنْ شَرِّ كُلِّ نَفْسٍ أَوْ عَيْنِ حَاسِدٍ اللَّهُ يَشْفِينِي بِاسْمِ اللَّهِ أَرْقِي نَفْسِي
Transliteration (for others - male): Bismillahi arqika, min kulli shay'in yu'dhika, min sharri kulli nafsin aw 'ayni hasidi, Allahu yashfika, bismillahi arqika

Transliteration (for others - female): Bismillahi arqiki, min kulli shay'in yu'dhiki, min sharri kulli nafsin aw 'ayni hasidi, Allahu yashfiki, bismillahi arqiki

Transliteration (for yourself): Bismillahi arqi nafsi, min kulli shay'in yu'dhini, min sharri kulli nafsin aw 'ayni hasidi, Allahu yashfini, bismillahi arqi nafsi

Translation: "In the name of Allah I perform ruqyah for you/myself from everything that harms you/me and from the eye of a jealous one. May Allah cure you/me and in the name of Allah I perform ruqyah for you/myself."

Reference: Sahih Muslim 2186, Book 39, Hadith 54

Step 3: Recite Quranic Verses

Recite the following verses with contemplation and belief: You can use the Manzil option of this app which includes additional verses from the Quran.

Al-Fatihah (The Opening)

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ ﴿١﴾ الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ ﴿٢﴾ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ ﴿٣﴾ مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ ﴿٤﴾ إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ ﴿٥﴾ اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ ﴿٦﴾ صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ ﴿٧﴾

Ayat al-Kursi (Verse of the Throne)

اللَّهُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ لَا تَأْخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوْمٌ لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ مَنْ ذَا الَّذِي يَشْفَعُ عِنْدَهُ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِ يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَيْءٍ مِنْ عِلْمِهِ إِلَّا بِمَا شَاءَ وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ وَلَا يَئُودُهُ حِفْظُهُمَا وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ
"Allah - there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer of existence. Neither drowsiness overtakes Him nor sleep. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. Who is it that can intercede with Him except by His permission? He knows what is before them and what will be after them, and they encompass not a thing of His knowledge except for what He wills. His Kursi extends over the heavens and the earth, and their preservation tires Him not. And He is the Most High, the Most Great." [Al-Baqarah: 255]

The Last Two Verses of Al-Baqarah

آمَنَ الرَّسُولُ بِمَا أُنْزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِنْ رَبِّهِ وَالْمُؤْمِنُونَ كُلٌّ آمَنَ بِاللَّهِ وَمَلَائِكَتِهِ وَكُتُبِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ لَا نُفَرِّقُ بَيْنَ أَحَدٍ مِنْ رُسُلِهِ وَقَالُوا سَمِعْنَا وَأَطَعْنَا غُفْرَانَكَ رَبَّنَا وَإِلَيْكَ الْمَصِيرُ ﴿٢٨٥﴾ لَا يُكَلِّفُ اللَّهُ نَفْسًا إِلَّا وُسْعَهَا لَهَا مَا كَسَبَتْ وَعَلَيْهَا مَا اكْتَسَبَتْ رَبَّنَا لَا تُؤَاخِذْنَا إِنْ نَسِينَا أَوْ أَخْطَأْنَا رَبَّنَا وَلَا تَحْمِلْ عَلَيْنَا إِصْرًا كَمَا حَمَلْتَهُ عَلَى الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِنَا رَبَّنَا وَلَا تُحَمِّلْنَا مَا لَا طَاقَةَ لَنَا بِهِ وَاعْفُ عَنَّا وَاغْفِرْ لَنَا وَارْحَمْنَا أَنْتَ مَوْلَانَا فَانْصُرْنَا عَلَى الْقَوْمِ الْكَافِرِينَ ﴿٢٨٦﴾

Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, and An-Nas (The Three Quls)

Recite each three times:

قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ ﴿١﴾ اللَّهُ الصَّمَدُ ﴿٢﴾ لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُولَدْ ﴿٣﴾ وَلَمْ يَكُنْ لَهُ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ ﴿٤﴾
قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ ﴿١﴾ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ ﴿٢﴾ وَمِنْ شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ إِذَا وَقَبَ ﴿٣﴾ وَمِنْ شَرِّ النَّفَّاثَاتِ فِي الْعُقَدِ ﴿٤﴾ وَمِنْ شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ ﴿٥﴾
قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ ﴿١﴾ مَلِكِ النَّاسِ ﴿٢﴾ إِلَهِ النَّاسِ ﴿٣﴾ مِنْ شَرِّ الْوَسْوَاسِ الْخَنَّاسِ ﴿٤﴾ الَّذِي يُوَسْوِسُ فِي صُدُورِ النَّاسِ ﴿٥﴾ مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ ﴿٦﴾

Important Instructions for Ruqyah Performance:

  • Adjusting Pronouns: When performing ruqyah on someone else, change "I seek refuge" to "I seek refuge for you" in the duas
  • Repetition: You may repeat any Quranic verse or dua as needed
  • Blowing Techniques: You may blow with or without spittle - both are permissible
  • Hand Placement: You may blow in your hands and place them on the person
  • Physical Contact: You may read while holding the person or read into their ear
  • Timing of Blowing: You may blow after every verse, after every surah, or after every page - whatever follows the Sunnah

Prophetic Duas for Healing

General Healing Dua

اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ النَّاسِ أَذْهِبِ الْبَاسَ اشْفِهِ وَأَنْتَ الشَّافِي لَا شِفَاءَ إِلَّا شِفَاؤُكَ شِفَاءً لَا يُغَادِرُ سَقَمًا
"O Allah, Lord of mankind, remove the hardship and heal, for You are the Healer. There is no healing except Your healing, a healing that leaves no sickness."

Dua for Protection

أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّاتِ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ
"I seek refuge in the perfect words of Allah from the evil of what He has created."

Dua for Removing Distress

لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ الْعَظِيمُ الْحَلِيمُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ رَبُّ الْعَرْشِ الْعَظِيمِ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ رَبُّ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَرَبُّ الْأَرْضِ وَرَبُّ الْعَرْشِ الْكَرِيمِ
"There is no god but Allah, the Magnificent, the Forbearing. There is no god but Allah, Lord of the Magnificent Throne. There is no god but Allah, Lord of the heavens, Lord of the earth, and Lord of the Noble Throne."

Complete Healing Dua (Sahih al-Bukhari)

اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ النَّاسِ أَذْهِبِ الْبَاسَ، اشْفِهِ وَأَنْتَ الشَّافِي، لاَ شِفَاءَ إِلاَّ شِفَاؤُكَ، شِفَاءً لاَ يُغَادِرُ سَقَمًا
Transliteration: Allahuma rabbi-nas adhhabal ba'sa, ashfi wa anta al-shafi, la shifa' illa shifa'uka shifa' la yughadiru saqama.

Translation: "O Allah, the Lord of the people! Remove the trouble and heal the patient, for You are the Healer. No healing is of any avail but Yours; healing that will leave behind no ailment."

Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari 5743, Book 76, Hadith 58

Protection from All Harm

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الَّذِي لَا يَضُرُّ مَعَ اسْمِهِ شَيْءٌ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَا فِي السَّمَاءِ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
Transliteration: Bismillah illadhi la yadurru ma'a ismihi shay'un fi'l-ard wa la fi'l-sama' wa huwa al-samee' ul-'aleem

Translation: "In the name of Allah with Whose name nothing can harm on earth or in heaven, and He is the All-Hearing, All-Knowing."

Reference: At-Tirmidhi (hasan saheeh ghareeb), classed as saheeh by Ibn al-Qayyim and al-Albaani

Seeking Refuge in Allah's Perfect Words

أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّاتِ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ
Transliteration: 'A'oodhu bi kalimaat Allaah al-taammah min sharri ma khalaq

Translation: "I seek refuge in the perfect words of Allah from the evil of that which He has created."

Reference: Sahih Muslim 2709

Protection for Children (As used by Prophet Ibrahim)

أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّةِ مِنْ كُلِّ شَيْطَانٍ وَهَامَّةٍ وَمِنْ كُلِّ عَيْنٍ لَامَّةٍ
Transliteration: Aoodhu bi kalimaat-illaahit taammati min kulli shaytaanin wa haammatin wa min kulli 'aynin laammah

Translation: "I seek refuge with the perfect words of Allah from every Shaytan and poisonous insect and from every envious eye."

Note: The Prophet ﷺ used to seek refuge for al-Hasan and al-Husayn with this dua, saying: "Your forefather (Ibrahim) used to seek refuge with it for Isma'il and Ishaq."

Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari 3191

Comprehensive Protection Dua

أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللهِ التَّامَّةِ مِنْ غَضَبِهِ، وَعِقَابِهِ، وَشَرِّ عِبَادِهِ، وَمِنْ هَمَزَاتِ الشَّيَاطِينِ، وَأَنْ يَحْضُرُونِ
Transliteration: Audhu bi kalimati' llahi't-tammati min ghadabihi wa iqabihi wa sharri ibadihi wa min hamazati' sh-shayatin wa an yahdurun

Translation: "I seek refuge in the perfect words of Allah from His wrath and punishment, from the evil of His slaves and from the evil promptings of the devils and from their presence."

Reference: Muwatta Malik, Book 51, Hadith 9

Healing from All Diseases

بِاسْمِ اللهِ يُبْرِيكَ وَمِنْ كُلِّ دَاءٍ يَشْفِيكَ وَمِنْ شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ وَمِنْ شَرِّ كُلِّ ذِي عَيْنٍ
Transliteration: Bismillahi yubreeka wa min kulli daa'in yashfeeka wa min sharri haasidin iza hasad wa min sharri kulli zhi-'ayn

Translation: "In the name of Allah, may He cure you from all kinds of illnesses and safeguard you from the evil of a jealous one when he feels jealous and from everyone with the evil eye."

Reference: Sahih Muslim, Book 26, 5424

Asking Allah for Healing (Recite 7 times)

أَسْـأَلُ اللهَ العَـظيـم، رَبَّ العَـرْشِ العَـظيـم أَنْ يَشْفِيَكَ
Transliteration: 'As'alullaahal-'Adheema Rabbal-'Arshil-'Adheemi 'an yashfiyaka

Translation: "I ask Almighty Allah, Lord of the Magnificent Throne, to make you well."

Instructions: Recite 7 times

Reference: At-Tirmidhi, Abu Dawud. See also Al-Albani, Sahih At-Tirmidhi 2/210

For Physical Pain and Discomfort

بِسْمِ اللهِ (3x)
أَعُوذُ بِاللهِ وَقُدْرَتِهِ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا أَجِدُ وَأُحَاذِرُ (7x)
Transliteration:
Bis-mil-laah (3 times)
'A‛oodhu bial-laahi wa qudratih min shar-ri maa ajidu wa uḥaadhir (7 times)

Translation:
"In the name of Allah." (three times)
"I seek refuge with Allah and His omnipotence from the evil of what I feel and that which I am wary of." (seven times)

Instructions: Place your hand at the site of the pain while reciting

Make Personal Dua

End with sincere personal dua in your own words, asking Allah for healing, protection, and guidance.

General Guidelines:

  • Perform ruqyah regularly, preferably after Fajr and before sleeping
  • Have complete trust and reliance on Allah
  • Continue with medical treatment alongside ruqyah
  • Be patient - healing comes from Allah in His perfect timing
  • If symptoms worsen during ruqyah, this may indicate the presence of jinn or magic - continue with more ruqyah
  • Remember that all healing comes from Allah alone
