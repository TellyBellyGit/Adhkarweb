Du'a for Light and Memorization

Seeking Divine Illumination for Memory, Guidance, and Salvation

The Spiritual Path for Light and Memorization

This page presents a spiritual sequence for seeking Divine Light, particularly for strengthening memory for Qur'an and Du'as, and for receiving illumination on the Day of Judgment. The practice follows the method we discussed: beginning with the Verse of Light as a reminder and inspiration, followed by the comprehensive Du'a for Light, and concluding with specific supplications for knowledge and memory.

1. The Verse of Light (Ayat an-Nur)

Begin by reciting and reflecting upon this magnificent verse that establishes Allah as The Source of All Light. This serves as a spiritual preparation and reminder of the ultimate source of the illumination we seek.

اللهُ نُورُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۚ مَثَلُ نُورِهِ كَمِشْكَاةٍ فِيهَا مِصْبَاحٌ ۖ الْمِصْبَاحُ فِي زُجَاجَةٍ ۖ الزُّجَاجَةُ كَأَنَّهَا كَوْكَبٌ دُرِّيٌّ يُوقَدُ مِن شَجَرَةٍ مُّبَارَكَةٍ زَيْتُونَةٍ لَّا شَرْقِيَّةٍ وَلَا غَرْبِيَّةٍ يَكَادُ زَيْتُهَا يُضِيءُ وَلَوْ لَمْ تَمْسَسْهُ نَارٌ ۚ نُّورٌ عَلَىٰ نُورٍ ۗ يَهْدِي اللهُ لِنُورِهِ مَن يَشَاءُ ۚ وَيَضْرِبُ اللهُ الْأَمْثَالَ لِلنَّاسِ ۗ وَاللهُ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ عَلِيمٌ
Translation: "Allah is the Light of the heavens and the earth. The example of His light is like a niche within which is a lamp, the lamp is within glass, the glass as if it were a pearly [white] star lit from [the oil of] a blessed olive tree, neither of the east nor of the west, whose oil would almost glow even if untouched by fire. Light upon light. Allah guides to His light whom He wills. And Allah presents examples for the people, and Allah is Knowing of all things."

Reference: Qur'an 24:35

2. Comprehensive Du'a for Light

After reflecting on the Verse of Light, recite this comprehensive supplication taught by the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) to Abdullah ibn 'Abbas (may Allah be pleased with him).

Primary Version

اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْ فِي قَلْبِي نُورًا، وَفِي لِسَانِي نُورًا، وَاجْعَلْ فِي سَمْعِي نُورًا، وَاجْعَلْ فِي بَصَرِي نُورًا، وَاجْعَلْ مِنْ خَلْفِي نُورًا، وَمِنْ أَمَامِي نُورًا، وَاجْعَلْ مِنْ فَوْقِي نُورًا، وَمِنْ تَحْتِي نُورًا، اللَّهُمَّ أَعْطِنِي نُورًا
Transliteration: Allāhumma-j`al fī qalbī nūran, wa fī lisānī nūran, waj`al fī sam`ī nūran, waj`al fī baṣarī nūran, waj`al min khalfī nūran, wa min amāmī nūran, waj`al min fawqī nūran, wa min taḥtī nūran. Allāhumma a`ṭinī nūran.

Translation: "O Allah, place light in my heart, light in my tongue, light in my hearing, light in my sight, place light behind me, light in front of me, light above me, and light below me. O Allah, grant me light."

Reference: Sahih Muslim

Extended Version

اللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْ فِي قَلْبِي نُورًا، وَفِي بَصَرِي نُورًا، وَفِي سَمْعِي نُورًا، وَعَنْ يَمِينِي نُورًا، وَعَنْ شِمَالِي نُورًا، وَأَمَامِي نُورًا، وَخَلْفِي نُورًا، وَفَوْقِي نُورًا، وَتَحْتِي نُورًا، اللَّهُمَّ أَعْظِمْ لِي نُورًا
Transliteration: Allāhumma-j`al fī qalbī nūran, wa fī baṣarī nūran, wa fī sam`ī nūran, wa `an yamīnī nūran, wa `an shimālī nūran, wa amāmī nūran, wa khalfī nūran, wa fawqī nūran, wa taḥtī nūran. Allāhumma a`ẓim lī nūran.

Translation: "O Allah, place light in my heart, light in my sight, light in my hearing, light on my right, light on my left, light in front of me, light behind me, light above me, and light below me. O Allah, make great for me my light."

Reference: Sahih Muslim

3. Du'a for Knowledge and Memory

After seeking comprehensive light, specifically ask Allah to benefit you from knowledge and strengthen your memory for Qur'an and beneficial supplications.

Du'a for Beneficial Knowledge

اللَّهُمَّ انْفَعْنِي بِمَا عَلَّمْتَنِي، وَعَلِّمْنِي مَا يَنْفَعُنِي، وَزِدْنِي عِلْمًا
Transliteration: Allāhumma-nfa'nī bimā 'allamtanī, wa 'allimnī mā yanfa'unī, wa zidnī 'ilmā.

Translation: "O Allah, benefit me by what You have taught me, teach me what is beneficial for me, and increase me in knowledge."

Reference: Sunan al-Tirmidhi

Du'a for Understanding and Retention

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ عِلْمًا نَافِعًا، وَرِزْقًا طَيِّبًا، وَعَمَلًا مُتَقَبَّلًا
Transliteration: Allāhumma innī as'aluka 'ilman nāfi'an, wa rizqan tayyiban, wa 'amalan mutaqabbalan.

Translation: "O Allah, I ask You for beneficial knowledge, wholesome provision, and accepted deeds."

Reference: Sunan Ibn Majah

Recommended Practice

Spiritual Sequence for Optimal Benefit:

  • Begin with the Verse of Light (Qur'an 24:35) - Recite slowly and reflect on its meaning as a reminder of Allah as The Source of All Light.
  • Recite the Comprehensive Du'a for Light - Ask for illumination in all aspects of your being and life.
  • Follow with the Du'a for Knowledge and Memory - Specifically request beneficial knowledge and strong retention.
  • Set Your Intention (Niyyah) - Consciously intend that this light will strengthen your memory for Qur'an and Du'as, and be your illumination on the Day of Judgment, especially on the Sirat.
  • Be Consistent - Make this practice part of your daily routine, ideally after prayers or during Tahajjud (pre-dawn) time.
  • Combine with Action - Continue your practical efforts to memorize while seeking Allah's blessing through this spiritual practice.

Note: The "Light" (Nur) mentioned in these supplications refers to divine guidance, faith, wisdom, clarity, and the spiritual illumination that leads to truth and protects from error. It encompasses both worldly guidance and otherworldly salvation.
