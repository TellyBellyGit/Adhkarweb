Islamic Spiritual Affliction Diagnostic Guide

Based on Quran, Sunnah, and Practical Experience

Important Disclaimer

This guide is for educational purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional medical or psychological care. Always rule out natural causes first before considering spiritual affliction. Consult with qualified Islamic scholars and Raqis for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Fundamental Principle: Start with the Natural

The first and most critical rule in Islamic diagnostics is to always rule out the natural and medical causes first. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "O slaves of Allah, seek treatment." (Sunan al-Tirmidhi).

Exhaust All Medical and Psychological Avenues
  • Medical Check-up: Many symptoms attributed to spiritual affliction (chronic fatigue, pain, seizures, digestive issues) have clear medical causes.
  • Psychological Evaluation: Consult a qualified psychologist or psychiatrist for symptoms like depression, anxiety, dissociative disorders, or hallucinations.

Only if thorough medical and psychological investigations yield no conclusive results, or symptoms have distinctly unnatural patterns, should you proceed with spiritual assessment.

Spiritual Assessment Framework

Category 1: Signs of Sihr (Magic/Sorcery)

Sihr is specific, targeted, and often has a "point of impact."

  • Sudden, Unexplained Aversion: Intense, irrational dislike for spouse, family, or home (Sihr al-Tafreeq)
  • Life Blockage (Sihr al-Ma'toon): Consistent failure in every endeavor despite efforts and du'as
  • Strange Physical Symptoms: Severe localized pain, sudden health changes after visiting specific places or receiving gifts
  • Disturbing Dreams: Being chased by animals, falling, or in dirty places
  • Environmental Findings: Discovering knots, threads, powders, or buried objects

Category 2: Signs of Al-Ayn (The Evil Eye)

Caused by jealousy and admiration, often unintentional.

  • Sudden Deterioration After Praise: Affliction strikes shortly after being praised without "Masha'Allah"
  • Targeting Blessings: Affliction targets specific prized blessings (beauty, wealth, relationships)
  • Rapid Onset: Dramatic effect shortly after praise or admiration
  • Symptoms: Feeling "drained," unexplained sadness, anxiety, isolation

Category 3: Signs of Jinn Possession or Influence (Mass)

Ranges from whisperings to full possession.

  • Whisperings (Waswas): Intrusive negative thoughts during prayer, unfounded doubts in faith
  • Oppression (Qahr): Nightmares, sleep paralysis, feeling watched, hearing voices
  • Possession (Taskhallus): Complete personality change, superhuman strength, knowledge of unseen, violent reactions to Quran

The Ruqyah and Audio Response Test

The ultimate diagnostic tool from the Sunnah is the Ruqyah itself. Reactions to Islamic recitations provide critical diagnostic information.

Adhan (Call to Prayer) Response Test

Adhan
Adhan
Adhan

Play the Adhan and carefully observe your inner reactions and thoughts. This isn't just about listening — it's about witnessing your spiritual state.

Watch for These Resistance Patterns:

Mental Resistance:
  • Feeling irritated, bored, or impatient
  • Thinking "this is a waste of time" or "it's taking too long"
  • Judging that "it's silly to play Adhan outside prayer time"
  • Suddenly remembering urgent tasks that feel overwhelmingly important
Behavioral Avoidance:
  • Criticizing the reciter's voice or audio quality
  • Jumping between different Adhan recordings without completing any
  • Finding excuses to stop before the Adhan finishes

The Challenge:

If you experience these negative thoughts, try to push through them and listen to one complete Adhan (approximately 2.5 minutes). Then, immediately listen to another complete Adhan and observe if the resistance intensifies or diminishes.

Why This Matters:

The ability to simply listen to the Call to Allah without internal resistance is a powerful indicator of your spiritual openness. Shaytan often uses seemingly "reasonable" distractions to pull you away from remembrance of Allah.

Note:

If you can complete this exercise with focus and peace, that's an excellent sign of spiritual health. If you struggle intensely, it may indicate spiritual blockage that requires attention.

Observed Reactions:

Normal/Healthy

Feeling of tranquility, peace, longing for prayer, repeating words

Mild Affliction

Irritation, restlessness, anxiety, desire to leave room

Moderate Affliction

Headache, chest tightness, drowsiness specifically during Adhan

Strong Jinn Indication

Covering ears violently, screaming, cursing the Adhan, seizures

Quranic Recitation Response Test

Perform Ruqyah Ash-Shar'iyyah in this sequence:

Now, you will listen to specific Quranic verses used in Ruqyah (Quranic healing). The reactions here can be more pronounced than with the Adhan, as these verses are a direct challenge to any negative spiritual influence.

Instructions for this Test:

Play the first set of verses (Surah Al-Fatihah, Ayat al-Kursi, Last two verses of Surah Al-Baqarah).

Observe yourself closely. In addition to all the signs of resistance and aversion you noted during the Adhan test, be especially alert for the following, which are stronger indicators:

  • Physical Discomfort: A sudden headache, feeling nauseous, specific stabbing or burning pains in any part of the body, excessive yawning, or feeling heavily drowsy.
  • Intense Emotional Shifts: Unexplained anger, intense sadness, fear, or the urge to cry without feeling connected to Allah.
  • Mental Blockages: An inability to focus on the words, your mind going completely blank, or being flooded with violent or blasphemous thoughts.
  • Aversion to Continuing: A powerful, compelling feeling that you must stop the audio immediately.

If you experience strong negative reactions, note them down but try to push through and complete the recitation if you can. The struggle itself is diagnostic.

If you experience no negative reactions, or even feel a sense of peace, calm, or lightness, this is a very positive sign. Continue listening to the remaining verses and observe if this feeling strengthens.

Important: The purpose of this test is observation and diagnosis. If you experience severe physical reactions (like convulsions) or intense psychological distress, stop immediately and seek guidance from a qualified and trusted Raqi or Islamic scholar. Do not attempt to treat severe reactions on your own.

    You will now listen to Surah Al-Falaq and Surah An-Nas. These two Surahs, revealed together, are a specific divine remedy from Allah against spiritual evils. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said: "No one seeks refuge in anything like these two." (Sunan an-Nasa'i)

    Pay extremely close attention here. The reaction to these specific Surahs is often the most telling sign of all.

    What to Look For:

    While you should still watch for all previous signs (aversion, physical discomfort, etc.), the Al-Mu'awwidhat can trigger very specific reactions:

    • During Surah Al-Falaq (The Daybreak):

      This Surah specifically seeks protection from the evil of black magic (sihr). A strong, specific reaction here—like a sudden sharp pain, intense heat, or overwhelming agitation. This is a powerful indicator of sihr being present.

    • During Surah An-Nas (Mankind):

      This Surah specifically seeks protection from the whisperings of Jinn and mankind. A strong reaction here—like feeling choked, hearing internal voices arguing, or a surge of fear points strongly to Jinn influence (waswas or oppression).

    Instructions:

    Listen to Surah Al-Falaq first. Observe your reactions with full focus. Note any physical sensations, emotional shifts, or intrusive thoughts during and immediately after the recitation.

    Then, listen to Surah An-Nas. See if the reaction is different. Does it get stronger? Weaker? Does the type of discomfort change?

    Compare and Contrast. The difference in your reaction between these two Surahs can help differentiate between an affliction of magic (Sihr) and one of Jinn whisperings or possession.

    Consider this the most important part of your self-assessment. A violent aversion or physical reaction to the Al-Mu'awwidhat is one of the clearest signs from the Sunnah that a genuine spiritual affliction is present and that you should seek the help of a qualified Raqi.

A Note on Repeating These Tests

You may repeat these diagnostic sessions as needed, but please adhere to the following guidelines to ensure you do not fall into doubt or spiritual anxiety:

For Confirmation

If your initial results were unclear, you may repeat the tests after 24 hours. This allows time for your spiritual and emotional state to settle.

To Track Progress

If you are undergoing treatment (like Ruqyah or spiritual therapy), repeating these tests once a week can help you observe your progress. Look for a reduction in the intensity of your negative reactions over time.

Crucial Warning - Do Not Obsess

Do not repeat the tests multiple times in a single day. If you find yourself constantly wanting to re-check, feeling that a single "clean" test is never enough, or becoming anxious if you skip a day, this is likely a sign of waswasah (devilish whisperings). In this case, the act of testing itself is becoming a problem. Stop the tests immediately and focus instead on consistent daily dhikr (remembrance of Allah) and worship.

Focus on the Cure, Not Just the Diagnosis

Remember, the goal is to seek healing through the Quran and Sunnah. If the tests indicate a problem, shift your focus from endless diagnosis to the consistent practice of remedies—such as daily recitation of the Adhkar, listening to the Quran for healing, and performing Ruqyah upon yourself as prescribed.

A Balanced Approach

  • Initial Diagnosis: Perform the tests once.
  • Confirmatory Test: If needed, repeat after a day.
  • Shift to Treatment: Begin your prescribed spiritual regimen.
  • Progress Check: Repeat the tests no more than once a week to gauge improvement.

This balanced approach protects you from the harm of obsession while allowing you to benefit from the diagnostic process.

Why this framing is essential

  • Prevents Harm: It directly addresses the primary risk of self-diagnosis in spiritual matters: obsessive-compulsive behavior, which Shaytan exploits.
  • Provides a Clear Protocol: It gives users a safe, structured "permission" to repeat the tests without leaving it open to interpretation.
  • Promotes Action: It guides the user from a state of passive diagnosis ("What's wrong with me?") to active treatment ("This is how I will get better").
  • Aligns with Islamic Principles: It encourages trust in Allah (Tawakkul) after taking appropriate measures, rather than constant doubt and re-checking.

Final Counsel and Warnings

  • Do Not Obsess: The greatest trick of Shaytan is to make a healthy person believe they are afflicted.
  • Strengthen Your Foundation: Ensure your prayers are on time, recite morning and evening adhkar, and earn Halal income.
  • Seek a Qualified Raqi: If self-assessment points to affliction, stop self-diagnosing and seek a knowledgeable, trustworthy Raqi.
  • Tawakkul is the Key: Place complete trust in Allah. "And if Allah should touch you with adversity, there is no remover of it except Him." (Quran, 6:17).
